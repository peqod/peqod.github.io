Cultural institutions sit on places that deserve photorealistic digital twins — and get either heavyweight mesh viewers, app installs, or nothing. [Tosee](https://www.tosee.art/) is a browser-native 3D visualization platform that lets institutions capture, distribute, and monetize photorealistic models of heritage sites. No install. A URL.

## What I built

- Architected the full rendering pipeline: photogrammetry → 3D Gaussian Splatting training → SOGS compression → real-time WebGL delivery via Three.js.
- Delivery is **20× smaller than traditional mesh** while preserving archival-grade fidelity — it runs on an iPhone 12.
- Full product design: identity, UI/UX, and the asset pipeline from raw capture to published scene.

## Where it stands

Currently onboarding two major institutions — the **Royal Castle** and the **National Museum in Warsaw**.

The interesting part is the bridge: frontier graphics research (Gaussian Splatting, WebGL optimization) applied to cultural preservation, so heritage sites become immersive, accessible, and permanent.
